The MedTech market is developing rapidly, and the MedTech market in India is projected to grow to $50 billion by 2030 because of policy support, hospital growth, and rapid integration of AI into healthcare.<sup>1</sup>

But hiring has not kept up. There is a widening gap between the number of roles opening up, the availability of skilled talent, and the speed at which companies need to hire. That’s why many teams are facing the same challenges: roles stay open longer, [candidates drop off](https://www.thetalentpool.ai/blogs/how-to-reduce-candidate-drop-offs/) faster, and hiring quality becomes harder to maintain.

In this guide, you can find the breakdown of what is actually going on in medtech hiring, where skills and roles are needed, and what hiring strategies are working in the market in 2026, so you can develop a process that keeps up with the market.

## **What is Driving MedTech Hiring Growth in 2026?**

A convergence of market growth, policy push, and fast technology adoption is driving MedTech hiring in 2026. The Economic Times reports that the MedTech industry in India is expanding at a rate of about 27% CAGR, which is indicative of the type of growth that not only leads to the creation of jobs but also a sense of urgency.<sup>1</sup>

Here are the top drivers behind the rapid growth of MedTech Hiring in India:

- Hospital infrastructure is expanding fast.
- Government initiatives like PLI and MedTech parks
- Public healthcare expansion (PMJAY)

The result? 88% of healthcare recruiters expected to add headcount in H1 2026.<sup>2</sup>

## **Why are the Biggest Challenges in MedTech Hiring?**

### **Skill Shortages**

The biggest challenge in MedTech hiring today is not demand. It is supply. 82% of employers report difficulty finding the right talent.<sup>3</sup>

And this shortage is sharper in MedTech because roles require a rare mix of:

- Domain knowledge (healthcare, devices)
- Technical expertise (AI, software, engineering)
- Regulatory understanding

So, as a recruiter, you are not hiring for one skill. You are hiring for intersections.

Specialized roles, especially in regulatory, clinical, and MedTech engineering—remain consistently hard to fill.

### **Long Hiring Cycles**

On top of that, hiring cycles are long:

- Mid-level roles: ~6–8 weeks
- Senior roles: 3–6 months

Slow processes mean lost candidates. The best talent won’t wait. So, by the time you make an offer, candidates often already have 2–3 others.

### **High Competition**

Tech companies, pharma firms, and even startups are after the same talent, particularly AI, data and product development roles. This is driving up expectations and creates pressure.

### **Regulatory Uncertainty**

The changing regulations and compliance standards in the healthcare industry are often subject to frequent changes that can make a quick shift in hiring priorities. Critical roles today might become less predictable and more complex tomorrow as workforce planning becomes more challenging.

## **What are the Most In-Demand Medical Technology Jobs?**

By 2026, the most sought-after medical technology jobs will not be in the traditional specialties, but in hybrid, tech-enabled jobs. Here’s where demand is highest:

### **Software & AI Roles**

AI is no longer optional in MedTech. It is core.

- AI/ML Engineers
- Data Scientists
- HealthTech Software Developers

### **Regulatory & Compliance Roles**

With new CDSCO regulations and AI-device frameworks, compliance hiring is surging.

- Regulatory Affairs Specialists
- Quality Assurance Engineers
- Clinical Data Managers

### **R&D And Product Engineering**

Still the backbone of MedTech.

- Biomedical Engineers
- Device Engineers
- Validation Specialists

### **Clinical & Market Access Roles**

A newer but critical category.

- Clinical Research Analysts
- Health Economists
- Market Access Specialists

As reimbursement models evolve, these roles are becoming strategic hires.

## **How Long Does the MedTech Hiring Process Take?**

Hiring timelines in MedTech are longer than most industries, and getting longer.

Typical benchmarks:

- Entry/mid-level: 45–60 days
- Senior roles: 3–6 months

For niche roles like AI engineers or regulatory experts, timelines can stretch even further.

This is where most teams struggle. Because while hiring takes months, candidates make decisions in weeks. That gap is where candidate drop-offs happen.

Degrees don’t guarantee capability anymore.

To ensure [skill-based hiring](https://www.thetalentpool.ai/blogs/skill-based-hiring-the-new-hiring-trend-in-recruitment/), companies are prioritizing:

- Practical assessments
- Case-based evaluations
- Real-world problem solving

### **2\. Hire From Adjacent Industries**

The talent you need may not come from MedTech.

- Mechanical - Medical devices
- Software - HealthTech

## **How is Remote and Hybrid Hiring Changing MedTech?**

MedTech hiring is no longer restricted to metro cities. Around 55% of job seekers in India now prefer hybrid work.<sup>4</sup>

This is pushing companies to:

- Hire from Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities
- Build remote-first teams
- Tap into global talent pools

The movement towards flexible workforce models such as contract positions, [fractional talent](https://www.thetalentpool.ai/blogs/what-is-fractional-talent-why-is-it-trending/), and consultant positions is increasing. This hybrid model assists businesses to grow more rapidly without the burden of expenses in the long term.

## **What Role Do Regulations Play in Hiring?**

Regulation is no longer just a compliance function but a hiring driver. Recent changes include:

- CDSCO reforms
- Software-as-Medical-Device guidelines
- Data protection laws
- Health Technology Assessment (HTA) frameworks

These are creating demand for:

- Regulatory experts
- Compliance specialists
- Health economists

In short, hiring is not just about building products. It is about building compliant, market-ready products.
